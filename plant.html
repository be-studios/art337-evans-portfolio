Recycling Plant

Recyclables are combined to form a 6 ft tree. The “trunk” consists of 12 sealed biospheres growing grass with self-sustaining water cycles. I cut 24, 2-liter soda bottles and filled the bottom halves of the clear bottles with soil, water, and grass seed. I chose to use clear plastic for the biospheres so that observers could watch the natural growth. I passed a crocheted rope through the green bottles to represent organic water systems. The bottle caps were also color-coordinated. Green caps are used near the top and on the biospheres to represent the upwards direction of growth. The blue caps are used at the base/root area to symbolize water intake. I attached cardboard strips with holes punched in them to create boughs. I then tied soda cans and threaded curled-up paper through the holes to simulate fruits and branches.
